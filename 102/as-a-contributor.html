RTM: It’s a mindset shift toward Vertical Agile and Traceability. It ensures that every 'slice' we build is high-value and audit-ready from day one. It basically keeps the high-speed front end perfectly syncs all the way to the back-end ERP, exactly how the business intended.


🧱 My foundation is in HP and SAP, but my approach really evolved as I navigated the Salesforce revenue cycle.

👓 When I look at a transaction, I use three lenses: velocity for Sales, validation for the order, and finality for revenue recognition. My goal is to give the team that front-end speed without ever compromising the financial ledger.

❤️ I love solving that 'missing link' between sales agility and operational stability. I’m happy to dive into how MDST worked at Thrive, or we can start wherever you'd like. Where should we head first?"

MDST - Examples of Previous Project Work Explanation Framework

1. The Thrive Project (Non-Profit Optimization)

Business Shift

"Our macro goal was to completely operationalize a high-speed, student-run non-profit scaling across two universities. I needed to transition them from a culture of manual, ad-hoc guessing into a highly scalable, predictable sales motion to secure funding for local education."

Phase 1: Strategic Alignment (The "Walls")

"The North Star was protecting their 'Proceeds' margins with 100% certainty. However, we were constrained by a total lack of standardization. They were offering over 100 random event options, which was creating massive administrative overhead, unpredictable hard costs, and severe risk of surprise vendor bills wiping out their fundraising."

Phase 2: Technical Architecture (The "Build")

"To solve this, I designed a rigid 'Digital Pipeline.' I enforced a 'Thin Catalog' architecture using CPQ, restricting offerings to pre-configured event modules like 'Diamond Sponsorships.'

Crucially, I built a 'Cost Watchdog' workflow: I configured the system to place a hard physical block on the quoting of any custom stages or screens until the exact supplier costs were logged and system-approved, completely eliminating rogue spending."

Phase 3: Cognitive Evolution (The "Legacy")

"The biggest takeaway for me was realizing that even in a mission-driven non-profit, strict architectural guardrails are not a hindrance; automated margin protection is actually what enables the business to scale its mission safely."

2. Amazon Pharmacy / ATS (Complex Manufacturing)

Business Shift

"The business mandate was to drastically accelerate the sales and manufacturing lifecycle for ATS, which engineers massive, 11,000-part automated dispensing machines for Amazon Pharmacy."

Phase 1: Strategic Alignment (The "Walls")

"Our goal was velocity and compliance, but we were bottlenecked by a highly manual quoting process that took 97 steps. Reps were tracking parts in physical notebooks, making accurate costing impossible and delaying time-to-market."

Phase 2: Technical Architecture (The "Build")

"I architected a solution to digitize the Bill of Materials (BOM) through a unified quote record—what we called a digital 'Envelope.'

If a sales rep configured a machine with a net-new custom part, I built an automation that instantly routed a 'photo' of the requirement directly to the Supply Chain Management (SCM) team. The system enforced a hard stop on the quote generation until an approved cost was returned via deep integrations between the Sales and Supply Chain systems."

Phase 3: Cognitive Evolution (The "Legacy")

"This project proved how critical it is to architect systems that balance competing departmental needs: aligning the Sales team's demand for 'velocity' with the Supply Chain team's strict requirement for 'validation.' Ultimately, every machine shipped with a digital 'Passport' proving 100% compliance."

3. Clarivate (M&A Divestiture & Revenue Recognition)

Business Shift

"The executive agenda was to manage the complex platform and billing architecture required when Clarivate decided to divest one of its core products, ScholarOne, to Silverchair."

Phase 1: Strategic Alignment (The "Walls")

"The environmental constraint was severe: we had to surgically extract a single business unit out of a heavily integrated suite without causing a second of downtime for the end-user, and without breaking the back-office billing operations."

Phase 2: Technical Architecture (The "Build")

"Instead of attempting a rigid, monolithic rebuild, I designed a modular architecture centered around an 'Asset Tagging' system.

We simply updated the ownership tags on the ScholarOne assets. The end-user maintained a single login, while I utilized the automated Revenue Recognition (RevRec) engines to automatically intercept the customer's payment, split it logically, and route the correct funds directly to Silverchair, minus our automated management fee."

Phase 3: Cognitive Evolution (The "Legacy")

"My architectural philosophy evolved here: true enterprise scale isn't just about how seamlessly you can add and integrate products; it's about building a data model agile enough to seamlessly un-couple them when M&A business strategies shift."

4. Iron Mountain (Compliance & Service Bundling)

Business Shift

"We needed to build a modern quoting engine for InSight DXP, an AI medical scanning vault, while rigorously defending Iron Mountain’s legal classification as a Real Estate Investment Trust (REIT)."

Phase 1: Strategic Alignment (The "Walls")

"Our North Star was a unified quote, but the legal constraints were absolute: 75% of all revenue legally must come from real estate (the physical vault storage). When sales reps gave arbitrary discounts to win deals, they were accidentally reducing the real estate revenue bucket, actively jeopardizing the entire company's tax status."

Phase 2: Technical Architecture (The "Build")

"I designed a 'Dual-Nature' quoting model using advanced CPQ product bundling. I wrote system rules that automatically split every order invisibly into a 'Rent' bucket (storage) and a 'Work' bucket (AI analysis).

I locked the 'Rent' bucket at the system level. If a rep needed to apply a competitive discount, my pricing logic forced that discount entirely onto the 'Work' bucket, protecting the real estate margins."

Phase 3: Cognitive Evolution (The "Legacy")

"I learned how to act as a translator between legal and sales. An architect's job is to take incredibly complex corporate tax laws and code them into invisible, automated guardrails so the sales team can just focus on selling."

5. Trimble (Hardware/Software Sync & Compliance)

Business Shift

"The goal was to orchestrate the global quoting and fulfillment architecture for Trimble’s 'Best-of-Breed' construction suite, which requires blending physical hardware, remote satellite software, and strict local legal compliance."

Phase 1: Strategic Alignment (The "Walls")

"We were facing massive geopolitical liability and billing chaos. The critical bottleneck was that physical hardware was frequently shipping before satellite software licenses were legally cleared for use in specific, highly regulated geopolitical regions."

Phase 2: Technical Architecture (The "Build")

"I implemented a strict 'Contract First' architecture. I built a 6-Point Perfection check (KYC) that hard-blocked quoting until legal clearance was systematically verified.

Once signed, I utilized an Order Management System (OMS) as a 'Splitter and Stitcher.' The system automatically split the order to ship physical sensors via an Address Doctor validation, while simultaneously stitching the software activation to an Evergreen subscription model in the billing engine."

Phase 3: Cognitive Evolution (The "Legacy")

"The ultimate takeaway was proving to the business that enforcing strict discipline upfront during the quote phase is the only way to guarantee flawless, liability-free execution downstream in fulfillment and billing."

6. Komatsu (Guided Selling & Configuration)

Business Shift

"Following their acquisition of Joy Global, we needed to completely modernize the sales cycle for Komatsu’s massive mining crushers. We had to transform their go-to-market from a slow 'Design-to-Order' process into a rapid 'Configure-to-Market' motion."

Phase 1: Strategic Alignment (The "Walls")

"The operational reality was grim: generating a single quote took a month. Sales and Engineering were trapped in endless email loops trying to manually configure over 3,200 different machine variations without breaking technical dependencies."

Phase 2: Technical Architecture (The "Build")

"I extracted the tribal knowledge directly from the engineers and architected a 'Guided Genie' using CPQ Guided Selling logic.

I mapped 3,200 variations down to just three simple user inputs: rock density, volume throughput, and voltage. Based on those inputs, my configuration rules instantly generated the perfect, technically viable BOM."

Phase 3: Cognitive Evolution (The "Legacy")

"I learned that the best architecture doesn't just store data; it democratizes complex engineering knowledge, allowing the sales team to operate autonomously with incredible velocity, which in this case allowed Komatsu to build and ship machines 30% faster."

7. Pure Storage (IoT Integration & Asset Lifecycle)

Business Shift

"Our objective was to optimize the entire sales, manufacturing, and service lifecycle for Pure Storage's 100% Flash arrays as they transitioned to an 'Evergreen' subscription model."

Phase 1: Strategic Alignment (The "Walls")

"We were fighting a decade-long issue with data fidelity. If we ordered every exact screw in the ERP, we severely limited the manufacturer's (Foxconn) ability to optimize costs. But if we sent vague orders, it led to nightmare scenarios for our field service teams who didn't know exactly what was installed at the client site."

Phase 2: Technical Architecture (The "Build")

"I architected a 'Digital Asset' shell. We quoted only high-level components (L0-L2) in CPQ to give Foxconn supply chain flexibility.

The innovation was the feedback loop: when the physical array was installed, the hardware triggered an IoT 'Call Home' signal. I integrated this telemetry to automatically stitch the exact physical serial numbers back into the digital asset record in Salesforce."

Phase 3: Cognitive Evolution (The "Legacy")

"This project successfully bridged the gap between a physical piece of hardware and its digital 'twin' in the back-office system, providing the service team with a perfect picture of the client's architecture and automating our trial-to-order conversions."

8. Generic RLM (The Full Lifecycle - "Tesla" metaphor)

Business Shift

"The ultimate goal of this architecture is to transition a business from a transactional mindset to managing the complete, end-to-end Revenue Lifecycle of a complex physical asset tied to digital, usage-based subscriptions."

Phase 1: Strategic Alignment (The "Walls")

"The core bottleneck preventing modern companies from scaling is fragmentation. Quoting, financing, subscription billing, and asset modifications usually live in disconnected silos, creating a disjointed customer journey and massive revenue leakage."

Phase 2: Technical Architecture (The "Build")

"I design unified data models utilizing the full Revenue Lifecycle Management (RLM) suite.

The architecture captures the initial quote, converts the booking deposit into an Order, integrates with back-office financing, and activates recurring subscriptions. Crucially, I build the system to manage the asset after the sale—handling complex contract amendments, usage-based true-ups, and automated billing suspensions."

Phase 3: Cognitive Evolution (The "Legacy")

"My philosophy as an architect is that the job isn't done when the deal is signed. True enterprise architecture manages the entire 'story' of the customer relationship—every bill, service update, and contract change—for years after the initial sale until the asset is retired."

To explain the Thrive project through the lens of your MDST (Maturity, Discovery, Solution, and Transformation) framework, we have to look at it as a journey from a fragmented "SKU Jungle" to a high-integrity revenue engine. Thrive’s mission—where every dollar supports local education—meant that any "requirements drift" wasn't just a technical failure; it was a loss of charitable impact.

Here is the breakdown of the Thrive transformation:

Phase 1: Maturity & Vision (The Anchor)

------------------------------

When I stepped in, Thrive was suffering from margin erosion and extreme operational drag.

  • The Vision: Eliminate manual "margin hacking" and protect the 20% profitability floor required to fund their educational programs.
  • The Problem: They were trapped in a "SKU Jungle." Because their legacy system was static, they had created over 200 separate booth SKUs to handle every possible variation of an event.
  • The Result: Sales reps were "flying blind," manually calculating costs in Excel, which led to inconsistent discounting and a back-office that was constantly "cleaning up" after the deals were won.

Phase 2: Discovery (The "Show Me" Phase)

------------------------------

Using the MDST Discovery Template, I moved away from what the SMEs said they did and focused on what they actually clicked.

  • The Revelation: By watching a "Day in the Life" of a rep, I saw them toggling between Salesforce and a 5-year-old "Sales Pricing.xlsx" sheet.
  • The Gap: I identified a massive Data Gap (internal costs weren't in the system) and a System Gap (the standard price book couldn't handle the "if-then" math required for dynamic catering fluctuates).

Phase 3: Solution (The Architectural Pivot)

------------------------------

This is where we applied the "Walking Skeleton" approach. Instead of building 200 more objects, we collapsed the complexity into a "Thin Catalog."

  • Attribute-Based Pricing: We replaced the 200+ static SKUs with a single, smart bundle. We used OmniStudio Pricing Procedures to fetch real-time costs from the ERP.
  • The "Because" Rule in Action: We built an Automated Margin Uplift. Why? Because the system needs to maintain a 20% floor. Why? Because the CFO needs to protect the charitable margin.
  • Technical Shielding: When developers suggested custom Apex for this, I pointed to the RTM and insisted on Declarative Flows and Decision Matrices, ensuring the Pricing Ops team could maintain it long-term without needing a coder.

Phase 4: Transformation (The Guardrails)

------------------------------

The final step was ensuring the project didn't "drift" back into old habits after go-live.

  • The SLA Watchdog: We implemented an automated alert system that proactively flagged any quote that stayed in "Pending Approval" for more than 24 hours, protecting Sales Velocity.
  • Governance: We physically locked the "Generate Document" button. If the margin wasn't 20% (or approved by the VP), the rep simply couldn't send the quote.
  • The Outcome: We moved from a manual, error-prone mess to a system that was audit-ready from day one. Thrive saw a 100% reduction in manual margin calculations and a significant lift in realized deal profit.
