3. Discovery: The Margin Protection Bridge

This is your interview guide. You use this during the initial "As-Is" workshops to validate the pain points before you ever mention Revenue Cloud or Pricing Procedures.

Process / Domain Current State (As-Is)(Business Reality) Future State (To-Be)(Business Intent) Discovery Focus (The Bridge / What to Ask)
Cost Visibility Sales reps do not know the exact catering costs when quoting; they rely on outdated Excel cheat sheets. Real-time internal costs are surfaced directly in the quoting engine alongside list prices. Ask: "How often do catering costs fluctuate? Are reps blindly quoting packages without knowing if the event space will actually be profitable?"
Margin Calculation Reps manually calculate $(\text{Total} - \text{Cost}) / \text{Total}$ to see if they hit the 20% target. Mistakes are common. The system calculates the blended margin dynamically as products are added or discounted. Observe: "Watch a rep build a quote. How much time are they spending toggling between Salesforce and their calculator? What happens when a customer asks for a sudden 5% discount?"
Margin Correction If the margin is too low, reps manually add arbitrary "Service Fees" to the quote, annoying the buyer. The system silently uplifts the core product (Booth Space) to absorb the catering cost and hit the 20% floor. Interview: "Ask Sales Leadership: Do your customers push back more on 'hidden fees' or on a slightly higher, blended 'Booth Space' package price?"

Approval SLA: The VP of Sales does not have a defined timeframe for reviewing these exceptions, which could stall deal velocity. Audit Trail: No historical tracking of how many quotes required an override, making it impossible to analyze if costs are simply too high. Missing Workflows: No Advanced Approvals logic configured to fire when the Calculated_Margin__c drops below the target floor from the Decision Matrix.

5. The Solutioning - Conceptual Architecture

Domain Business Intent (To-Be) Process Solution Data Solution System Solution
Cost Basis System must always know the true cost of goods sold (COGS) before pricing the customer. Centralize all product and service costs into a single repository that updates dynamically. Utilize standard Cost__c objects or Revenue Cloud Cost Books linked to the Product Catalog. Salesforce Revenue Cloud Advanced (Product & Pricing Engine).
Margin Enforcement System must automatically adjust prices upward if a quote's blended margin falls below the target floor. Quote calculations happen invisibly; if the margin is too low, the core product price (e.g., Booth Space) is automatically uplifted to compensate for high variable costs (e.g., Catering). A Decision Matrix stores Target_Margin__c by Product_Family__c. Pricing Procedure executes the math. OmniStudio Pricing Procedures & Decision Matrices.

7. Data - The Information Architecture

8. Development - The Build Strategy

Capability / Feature Declarative (Low-Code/Config) Programmatic (Custom Code) Key Testing Scenario (UAT)
Cost Lookup Add a Lookup Action in the Pricing Procedure to fetch the $2,500 catering cost from the Cost Book. None required. Add "Catering" to a quote; verify the Procedure logs $2,500 in the background cost variable.
Margin Math & Uplift Formula Element: Calculate current margin. If $\frac{\text{Total Quote} - \text{Total Cost}}{\text{Total Quote}} < 0.20$, execute a conditional Calculation Step to increase the "Booth Space" list price until the 20% threshold is met. None required (OmniStudio handles complex math natively). Build a $10,000 quote with $8,500 in costs (15% margin). Verify the system auto-increases the Booth Space price to reach a $10,625 total quote (20% margin).
Discount Lock Configure Advanced Approvals rule: If a rep manually discounts the auto-calculated Net Price, trigger an approval chain. None required. Rep tries to manually apply a 5% discount to the newly uplifted Booth Space; verify the "Submit for Approval" button becomes mandatory.

10. The Governance Template (The Guardrails)

Domain Process Guardrail (The People) Technical Guardrail (The System) Center of Excellence (CoE) Review
Matrix Maintenance The Pricing Strategy team must review and update the Target Margin matrix 30 days before the start of a new fiscal quarter. Version Control: OmniStudio allows versioning of Decision Matrices. The new version automatically activates on Day 1 of the quarter. Quarterly audit to compare the system's "Target Margin" against the actual "Realized Margin" on closed-won deals.
Approval Overrides The VP of Sales is the only person authorized to approve a deal that drops below the 20% algorithmic floor. System physically locks the "Generate Document" and "E-Signature" buttons on the Quote until the Approval Status is 'Approved'. Monthly review of all bypassed quotes: Are our costs too high, or is the 20% target unrealistic for certain markets like universities?
