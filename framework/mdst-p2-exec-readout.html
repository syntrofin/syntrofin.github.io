Executive Read Out


Here is how you distill all ten of those architectural tables into a hard-hitting, 3-slide executive readout.

When you are standing in front of the CFO and the VP of Sales, they do not want to see a 50-row traceability matrix. They want to see that you understand their business problem, have a bulletproof technical solution, and know how to protect their investment.

Here is the exact structure to get your Revenue Cloud Advanced architecture approved.

Slide 1: The Business Reality & The Vision

(Audience Focus: The CFO & VP of Sales. Goal: Prove you understand the pain and establish the ROI.)

Headline: Protecting Margins on Complex Event Packages

  • The Current State (The Pain): * Sales reps are flying blind on variable costs (like the $2,500 Chapel Hill catering fee).

    • Manual margin calculations in Excel lead to errors and inconsistent discounting.

    • Result: Multi-product quotes frequently close below our 20% profitability floor.

  • The Gap: Standard quoting systems cannot dynamically adjust core product prices based on underlying cost fluctuations. We lack the data structure and the automation engine to enforce margin floors at scale.

  • The Vision (The Future State): * Zero manual cost calculations for the sales team.

    • Systemic, invisible margin enforcement (Target: 0% of deals close under 20% margin without explicit VP approval).

Slide 2: The Architectural Solution

(Audience Focus: IT Leadership & Operations. Goal: Explain the "How" without getting bogged down in code.)

Headline: Automated Margin Safeguarding via Revenue Cloud Advanced

  • Data Strategy: Centralize all real-time inventory and catering costs into a Salesforce Cost Book, feeding directly from the Financial ERP.

  • The Engine (OmniStudio Pricing Procedures): * We will replace static Price Books with a dynamic rules engine.

    • A Decision Matrix will store category-specific margin targets (e.g., 20% for Events).

    • If a quote’s blended margin drops below the target, a Formula Element will automatically uplift the core product (Booth Space) to absorb the cost and protect the margin.

  • The Business Benefit: Customers see a single, clean package price. Sales reps quote faster. The business guarantees its minimum profit.

Slide 3: Execution & Governance

(Audience Focus: The Steering Committee. Goal: Prove this won't turn into the Wild West after Go-Live.)

Headline: Protecting the Investment and Managing Change

  • The Guardrails (Advanced Approvals): * If a rep manually attempts to discount the dynamically calculated "safe" price, the system physically locks document generation.

    • Exceptions are automatically routed to the VP of Sales for one-click approval.

  • Business Ownership:

    • IT does not own the pricing rules. The Pricing Strategy team will own and update the Decision Matrix targets quarterly.

  • Next Steps to Build:

    • Sprint 1: Cost Data Migration & ERP Integration Blueprint.

    • Sprint 2: OmniStudio Pricing Procedure & Formula Build.

    • Sprint 3: Advanced Approvals & UAT.

Why this structure wins:

You took a highly technical feature (Formula Elements in a Pricing Procedure) and wrapped it in a narrative that speaks to revenue, efficiency, and governance.

This gives you a rock-solid narrative to present to stakeholders.
