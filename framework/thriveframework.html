Vendor Costing & Margin Protection Architecture

How RCA halts the quoting process to fetch external vendor costs, applies markups, and protects gross margins.

Process Step RCA Technical Action & Objects Used The Margin Protection Mechanism
1. Flagging Custom Components Core Objects: QuoteLineItem
The sales rep adds a "Custom Titanium Liner" to the quote. This specific Product2 record has a boolean flag checked: Requires_Vendor_Pricing__c = TRUE. 		Validation Rule: The system locks the quote document generation. The rep cannot send a PDF to the customer as long as any Quote Line has this flag checked and the Unit Cost is $0.00.
2. The Vendor RFQ (Request for Quote) Custom Object: Vendor_Bid__c
A Flow automatically generates a Vendor Bid record linked to the Quote Line. An email or API callout (via MuleSoft) is sent to the supplier requesting a 30-day locked price for the materials. 		Data Silo: The customer never sees this. This ensures your internal cost negotiations are strictly separated from the commercial quote.
3. Applying the Cost-Plus Markup Core Objects: QuoteLineItem
The vendor replies with a cost of $10,000. This is mapped directly to the UnitCost field on the Quote Line. 		Salesforce Pricing (BRE): A Pricing Procedure fires. It takes the $10,000 UnitCost and applies a standard 40% markup, automatically setting the customer-facing UnitPrice to $14,000.
4. Defending the Margin (Approvals) Core Engine: Advanced Approvals
The sales rep decides $14,000 is too high and applies a manual discount, dropping the price to $11,000. 		Margin Calculation: The Pricing Procedure recalculates the Gross Margin: ($11,000 Price - $10,000 Cost) / $11,000 = 9% Margin.

Because 9% is below the corporate floor of 30%, Advanced Approvals locks the quote and routes it to the VP of Sales for executive override.
