Alignment Workshop



Day 0 Alignment Workshop Agenda

In the consulting world, it often call this the "Straw Man" approach. Human psychology is funny: if you ask a room full of executives to draw their business processes on a whiteboard, you get blank faces and awkward silence. But if you put a slightly incorrect process map on the screen, they will immediately jump out of their chairs to tell you exactly why you are wrong and how they actually do it.

You want them to tear down the Straw Man. That is how you get the truth quickly.

Here is the exact Day 0 Alignment Workshop Agenda to present your pre-compiled process catalogue and taxonomy, avoid the blank faces, and lock in the scope for your Revenue Cloud implementation.

Duration: 2 Hours Audience: Executive Sponsors, Project Managers, and key Department Heads (VP of Sales, CFO, Deal Desk Lead). Goal: Lock the L1-L3 Process Map and define the boundaries of the Discovery phase.

Part 1: The North Star & KPI Alignment (30 Mins)

You do not start with technology. You start with the artifacts they sent you (the business case) to prove you understand their goals.

  • The Playback: "Based on the SOW and the documents you shared, your primary goals are stopping margin leakage and reducing quote turnaround time by 50%."

  • The Metric Lock: "If we deliver a perfect Revenue Cloud system, but margin doesn't improve, this project is a failure. Do we all agree that 'Realized Deal Margin' is our North Star metric?"

  • Why this matters: When they start arguing later about wanting custom UI colors, you point back to this metric and ask, "Does that feature protect margin?"

Part 2: Tearing Down the Straw Man (60 Mins)

This is the core of the meeting. You project your Excel spreadsheet or process map on the screen.

  • The Reveal: "We used industry standards (APQC) and your existing training manuals to map your Quote-to-Cash landscape. We have identified 35 Level 3 processes. Let's review them."

  • The Red Pen Exercise: Go down the list. Force binary decisions.

    • You: "Process 4.2 is 'Non-Standard Term Approvals.' Is this in scope for Phase 1?"

    • Sponsor: "Yes, but we actually call that 'Deal Desk Routing.'" (You update the name live on the screen).

    • You: "Process 6.4 is 'Subscription Co-Terming.' Are we fixing that now, or pushing it to Phase 2?"

    • Sponsor: "Push it. We don't have the data ready for that yet." (You mark it Out of Scope).

  • Why this matters: You just avoided spending 20 hours of discovery time on Co-Terming, all because you forced the decision on Day 0.

Part 3: Assigning the "Boots on the Ground" (30 Mins)

Now that you have a locked list of L3 processes that are 'In Scope', you need to know who to interview.

  • The SME Mapping: For every In-Scope L3 process, demand a name.

    • You: "For 'Margin Floor Calculations' (Process 2.3), who is the absolute best person in the company who understands the current pain points?"

    • Sponsor: "That would be Sarah in Pricing Ops."

    • You: "Great. Sarah is now mandated to attend the deep-dive Discovery session for that specific process next Tuesday."

The Outcome of Day 0

You walk out of this 2-hour meeting with:

  1. A locked scope (The "In Scope" L3 list).

  2. A Discovery Schedule (You know exactly which workshops to host and who needs to be in the room).

  3. Executive Confidence (They didn't have to stare at a blank page; they just had to edit your draft).
