Staged delivery

In Salesforce Revenue Cloud Advanced (RCA), handling staged delivery for multi-event sponsorships or long-running events is significantly more robust than in legacy CPQ. You move away from "Order and forget" to a managed Lifecycle.

To handle your specific scenario—where a sponsor buys two events at once but the "delivery" (marketing assets, physical booth setup, and lead reports) happens in stages—you use a combination of Dynamic Revenue Orchestrator (DRO)and Order Splitting.

1. Order Decomposition (The "Stages")

When the Sponsor signs a deal for "Event A (Spring)" and "Event B (Fall)," the system creates one Order. However, RCA uses Decomposition Rulesto break that single order into multiple Fulfillment Orders.

  • Fulfillment Order 1 (Spring):Contains the Booth and Logo for the first event.
  • Fulfillment Order 2 (Fall):Contains the Booth and Logo for the second event.

This allows your team to track the status of the Spring event as "Fulfilled" while the Fall event remains "In Progress."

2. Orchestration Plans (The "Steps")

Within each fulfillment order, DROmanages the specific steps (Orchestration Items) required to deliver the value. For an event sponsor, these stages often look like this:

3. Revenue Recognition & Billing Ties

Because the delivery is in stages, you don't want to bill the sponsor for everything at once, or you may need to recognize revenue only as each stage is completed.

  • Milestone Billing:In RCA, you can tie the Billing Policyto the Orchestration Item. For example: "Invoice 50% upon Logo Approval" and "Invoice 50% upon Booth Handover."
  • Assetization:Only after the "Booth Construction" step is marked complete in DRO does the system "Assetize" the product, which can then trigger the final billing and revenue recognition for that specific stage.

4. Handling Long Events (Extended Delivery)

If an event lasts for 3 months, you can use Usage-Based Selling Modelsfor the ongoing services (like daily booth cleaning or concierge support). The system will keep the fulfillment order open and generate "Billing across the stage" based on periodic usage uploads.

Money Going Out

In an event management context within Salesforce Revenue Cloud Advanced (RCA), the costs you mentioned—Dinner & Drinks, Live Bands, and Venue Rental—represent the "Buy" side of the house.

To manage these effectively alongside your revenue, you need to bridge the gap between Salesforce RLM(Revenue Lifecycle Management) and your ERP/Procurementsystem. Here is how to architect this:

1. Internal Cost Components (The "COGS" Strategy)

Drawing on your experience with cost allocation for REIT compliance(like your work at Iron Mountain), you can treat these expenses as Internal Cost Linesattached to your Customer Quote.

  • The "Tagging" Method:Every time a sponsor buys a "Platinum Package," the system can automatically associate "Internal Cost" lines for:
    • Catering:Estimated per-head cost for dinner.
    • Entertainment:Fixed cost for the local band.
    • Venue:Fractional cost of the hall rental.
  • The Object:Use a custom Cost Componentobject linked to theQuote Line Item. This allows you to calculate Gross Marginin real-time before the Sales Rep even sends the quote to the sponsor.

2. Handling "Dynamic" Costs (The Live Band & Venue)

Unlike a standard product, the cost of a "Live Local Band" or a "Venue" might fluctuate.

  • Service-Based Products:Create these as "Internal-Only" products in your catalog.
  • Attribute-Driven Expenses:Use Attributesto drive the cost.
    • Attribute:"Band Tier" (Local, Regional, National).
    • Attribute:"Overtime Hours."
  • The Matrix:Use a Decision Matrixto look up the cost. If "Tier = Local" and "Duration = 4 hours," the matrix returns an internal cost of $2,000, which is then subtracted from the Revenue on your Profitability Dashboard.

3. Fulfillment vs. Procurement (DRO Integration)

This is where Dynamic Revenue Orchestrator (DRO)becomes powerful. You aren't just delivering a logo to a website; you are triggering a procurement workflow:

  • Orchestration Step - "Secure Venue":Once the Event Order is "Activated," DRO sends an outbound message to your ERP (e.g., SAP or Oracle) to create a Purchase Requisitionfor the venue.
  • Orchestration Step - "Contract Band":A task is generated for the Event Operations team to sign the contract for the local band.
  • Dependency Logic:The "Exhibitor Setup" stage cannot start until the "Venue Secured" orchestration item is marked as Complete.

4. Expense Recovery (The "Sponsor-Pays" Model)

If you are passing these costs directly to a specific sponsor (e.g., "Dinner Sponsored by Google"), you use Pass-Through Pricing:

  • Markup Logic:You take the $5,000 catering bill and apply a Pricing Procedurerule that adds a 15% management fee.
  • Billing:The Billing Policyin RCA is set to "Bill on Fulfillment." As soon as the Catering Company sends the final invoice and it's uploaded into the system, RCA generates the invoice for the Sponsor.

1. The Taxation Architecture

  • Tax Engine (Connector):RLM uses a standard integration to call external tax services during the Quote and Billing stages.

  • Tax Code Mapping:Each product (e.g., "Live Band") is mapped to a specific Tax Code (e.g., "Entertainment Services") that the tax engine recognizes.

Troubleshooting Guide: Failed SLA Billing Adjustments

Step 1: Check the "Lifecycle Managed" Flag

In Revenue Cloud Advanced (RLM), for an amendment to affect a billing schedule, the Asset or Order Item must have theLifecycle Managedflag set to True.

  • The Symptom:You create the credit amendment, but the original Billing Schedule remains at the full amount.
  • The Fix:Ensure your Product configuration allows for "Change Management" and that the initial Order was assetized correctly.

Step 2: Inspect the Revenue Transaction Error Log

RLM has a specific log for financial automation failures.

  • Where to look:Search for the Revenue Transaction Error Logobject in the App Launcher.
  • Common Error: "The Billing Schedule is currently Locked by a finalized Invoice."
  • The Architect's Insight:If you’ve already generated the invoice for that milestone, the system cannot "reach back" and change the schedule. You must issue a Credit Memoagainst the Invoice instead of an Adjustmenton the Schedule.

Step 3: Validate the Billing Treatment Filter

Every Billing Policyhas a Treatmentthat defines whichproducts it applies to.

  • The Symptom:The credit is recorded on the Order, but the Billing Engine ignores it.
  • The Check:Does your Billing Treatment filter include the "Credit" or "Adjustment" product code? If the filter is too narrow (e.g., only looking for "Hardware"), it will ignore the "SLA Penalty" line item you just added.

Step 4: Verify the "Effective Date" Logic

The timing of the SLA penalty must fall within the current Billing Period.

  • The Logic:If the event ended on June 1st and your billing period for the final milestone is June 15th, an adjustment dated June 20th will not be picked up by the current schedule.
  • The Fix:Ensure the Effective Dateof your SLA Amendment Order is set to a date beforetheBilling Target Date.
